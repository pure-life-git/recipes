# Citrus Braised Pork

## Ingredients

- 3 lb pork shoulder/butt, fat cap trimmed
- 2 tbsp + 1/3 c vegetable oil
- 2 medium oranges, peeled in large strips, juiced
- 5 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1 3" piece ginger, finely chopped
- 1/3 c soy sauce
- 1/4 c mirin
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
- 3 large shallots, thinly sliced

## Procedure

1. Cut the pork shoulder into 4 equally sized pieces. Pat dry and season on all sides with salt. Let sit for at least 4 hours, up to 24 hours.
2. Heat 2 tbsp of vegetable oil in a dutch oven over medium heat. Patting each dry beforehand, sear all pieces of the pork until a golden brown crust forms. Set aside
3. Into the dutch oven, add orange peels, orange juice, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, mirin, brown sugar, red pepper flakes, and 2 cups of water. Mix to combine everything.
4. Bring this mixture to a boil and then add the pork pieces back into the pot. Cover the pot, reduce the heat to a simmer (or put into a 325F oven), and cook for 2 hours. Remove the lid and continue to cook for an addition hour, until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened.
5. While the pork is cooking, prepare the topping by heating 1/3 c vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium high heat. Add the shallots in, stirring occassionally until golden brown, and then transfer to a paper towel to dry.
