# Al Pastor

## Ingredients

- 3 tbsp paprika
- 2 tbsp salt
- 1 tbsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tbsp ground coriander
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 1/4 tsp ground cloves
- 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
- 4 lb boneless pork shoulder
- 20 oz pineapple chunks, separated from juice
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 1/2 c apple cider vinegar
- 1/2 c fresh cilantro, chopped

## Procedure

1. In a small bowl, combine all the spices. Pat the pork dry and use a knife to cut a bunch of slits all over the pork. Fill these slits with the pineapple chunks.
2. Rub the pork shoulder with the spice mix, along with the onion and garlic. Place uncovered on a wire rack in the fridge for 4-24 hours.
3. Preheat an oven to 450F. Place the pork into a dutch oven and surround with apple cider vinegar, pineapple juice, 1 cup water, and any remaining pineapple chunks. Cover and place in the oven, reducing the heat to 300F, and cook until fork tender, about 3 hours.
4. Uncover, raise the oven temperature to 450F and cook until the pork forms a golden brown crust, 15-25 minutes. Remove from oven and shred with 2 forks, allowing to marinate in the juices for 10 minutes before serving with chopped cilantro.
