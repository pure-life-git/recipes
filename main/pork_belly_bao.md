# Pork Belly Bao

## Ingredients

- 1 c milk, warm
- 2 tbsp active dry yeast
- 8 tbsp sugar, divided (4 tbsp + 4 tbsp)
- 4 tbsp vegetable oil
- 5 c flour (600g)
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 4 lb pork belly, 1" cubes
- 1 large onion, diced
- 15 cloves garlic, grated
- 1 c chicken stock
- 1/4 c oyster sauce
- 1/4 c hoisin sauce
- 1/4 c dark soy sauce
- 1/4 c honey
- 1/4 c shaoxing or rice wine
- 2 c water
- 3 cucumbers, julienned
- 3 red chiles, thinly sliced
- 1 1/2 c cilantro leaves

## Procedure

1. In a glass measuring cup, combine 1/3 cup warm water, warm milk, active dry yeast, 4 tbsp sugar, and vegetable oil and let sit until foamy.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and 4 tbsp sugar. Pour in the yeast mixture and mix until a cohesive dough forms.
3. Dump the dough onto a lightly floured work surface and knead until no longer sticky. Place in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm spot until doubled in size.
4. Turn out the dough onto a lightly floured work surface and roll out to 1/4" thick. Cut into 3 1/2" circles and roll each out into an oval.
5. Grease one side of each dough oval and fold in half, pressing both halves together. Set on individual pieces of parchment and cover. Let rest for 30 minutes.
6. Steam the bao in a steamer basket for 8-12 minutes. Open the lid slightly once they're done and allow them to sit for 2-3 minutes before removing.
7. To make the pork belly filling, start by heating some olive oil in a large dutch oven over medium heat. Sear the pork belly cubes on all sides.
8. Add in the onion and the garlic and cook until the onion turns translucent.
9. In a separate bowl, combine the chicken stock, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, dark soy sauce, honey, and wine. Pour into the dutch oven, making sure to scrape the bottom as you do so.
10. Pour in the water and bring to a boil. Keep the mixture at a boil and cook until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened, about 20 minutes.
11. Slice a bao open, fill with pork belly, and top with cucumbers, cilantro, and chili.
