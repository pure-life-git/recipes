# Dutch Apple Crumble Pie

## Ingredients

- 1 pie crust

### For the filling

- 1/4 tsp lemon zest
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 1/2 lbs apples, cored, peeled, and 1/8" cubed
- 1/2 c brown sugar, packed (100g)
- 1/2 c sugar (100g)
- 1/3 c flour (40g)
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- 3/4 tsp ground nutmeg
- 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

### For the crumble topping

- 1 1/2 c flour (180g)
- 1/3 c sugar (75g)
- 3/4 c brown sugar (150g)
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 3/4 c butter, melted

## Procedure

1. In a medium saucepan, add the lemon zest, lemon juice, sliced apples, brown sugar, sugar, flour, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cardamom and set over medium heat.
2. Once everything is mixed together, cover with a lid and leave for 20 minutes, mixing every 3 minutes or so, until the apples are fork tender.
3. Remove from heat and mix in the vanilla extract. Transfer to a bowl or sheet tray and set aside to cool, either in the fridge or the freezer.
4. Roll out the pie crust and lay into a pie pan, folding over and crimping the edges. Place in the freezer until ready. While the crust is chilling, preheat the oven to 425 F and place a baking sheet on the middle rack.
5. While everything is chilling/preheating, make the sugar crumble. Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and combine with a fork until crumbly with some large chunks. Store in fridge until ready.
6. Once everything is properly chilled, spoon the apple filling into the shell and top with the crumble, only using enough to cover the apple filling.
7. Bake for 15 minutes at 425 F, then reduce heat to 350 F and bake for an additional 10 minutes.
8. Remove the pie from the oven, sprinkle the remaining crumble over top, place a foil cover over the edge of the crust, and return to the 350 F oven for an additional 25–35 minutes, until the edge of the crust is brown and the crumble is golden brown in the center.
9. Let the pie cool for at least 4 hours, and serve!
