# Claire Saffitz Meyer Lemon Tart

## Ingredients

- 3/4 c sugar (150g)
- Zest of 2 meyer lemons
- 5 large egg yolks
- 1 large egg
- 3/4 c lemon juice, 5-6 lemons worth
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 c butter, cubed, chilled
- 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 1/3 c flour (160g)
- 1/4 c powdered sugar (28g)
- 1/2 c whole milk greek yogurt
- 1/3 c raspberry jam

## Procedure

1. In the bowl of a food processor, add the flour, powdered sugar, and 1/2 tsp salt. Separately, in a small bowl, combine 1 egg yolk, 1/2 tsp vanilla extract, and 4 tsp water.
2. Pulse the food processor to combine the dry ingredients. Add in 1/2 cup of butter and pulse until the mixture reaches a sandy texture. Add the egg mixture and pulse until a dough forms. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
3. Cut the dough in half, leaving slightly more on one side. Use the smaller half to line the edges of a tart pan and the larger half to line the bottom. Make sure both the bottom and sides are an even thickness. Place in the freezer until firm, 15-20 minutes.
4. Trim any excess dough from the edge, line the bottom with foil, and bake at 350F for 15 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking for 20-15 minutes, until uniformly golden brown.
5. In a small saucepan, combine the sugar, lemon zest, and 1/2 tsp salt. Rub together with your fingers until fragrant.
6. Add in 1 egg and 4 egg yolks and blanch. Whisk in the lemon juice and set over medium heat, whisking constantly until the mixture reaches 170F.
7. Take the mixture off the heat and stir in the remaining 1/2 cup butter one piece at a time. Mix in the vanilla extract.
8. Cool in the fridge, using a piece of plastic wrap against the surface to prevent a skin from forming.
9. To assemble, spread a thin layer of raspberry jam along the bottom of the pie crust and bake at 350F for 5 minutes. While the crust is in the oven, whisk the greek yogurt into the lemon curd.
10. Fill the crust with the lemon curd and smooth out the top. Bake for 30 minutes at 350F, until the edges are set and the middle is slightly wobbly. Cool at room temp and chill for at least one hour before serving.
