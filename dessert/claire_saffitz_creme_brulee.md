# Claire Saffitz Creme Brulee

## Ingredients

- 3/4 c sugar (150g)
- 4 c half and half (960g)
- 1 vanilla bean pod, de-seeded
- 6 large egg yolks, room temp
- 2 large eggs, room temp
- 1/2 c demerara sugar (100g)

## Procedure

1. In a medium saucepan, combine the half and half, the seeds of the vanilla bean, the vanilla bean pod, and 1 tsp of salt. Set over medium heat and cook just until the mixture begins to simmer, whisking occasionally to break up clumps of vanilla seeds.
2. While the half and half mixture is heating up, in a medium bowl, blanch the egg yolks and eggs with the sugar. While constantly whisking, slowly stream the hot half and half mixture into the eggs. Continue whisking until cooled and sugar has dissolved. Optionally strain into a different bowl.
3. Pour the custard into 8 6oz ramekins and cover with tin foil, poking holes to let steam escape.
4. Line a large baking dish with a towel and place the ramekins on top, ensuring they stay level and are stable. Fill the baking dish with boiling water till it reaches about halfway up the ramekins.
5. Place the baking dish with the ramekins in a 300F oven and bake until the custard is set around the edges and slightly wobbly in the center, 30-35 minutes.
6. Let the ramekins rest in the baking dish for about 5 minutes and then transfer to a wire rack to cool to room temp. They can then be placed in the fridge to chill for at least 4 hours until ready to serve.
7. Once ready to serve, evenly sprinly 1 tsp of demerara sugar on top of each custard. Then, using a kitchen torch, carefully caramelize the sugar until you have a crackly top.

[!TIP] In order to not melt the custard underneath the sugar, move the torch around while bruleeing. Keep it about 6-10 inches away from the custard and move it in small circles.
