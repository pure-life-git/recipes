# Claire Saffitz Chocolate Souffle

## Ingredients

- 7 tbsp sugar, divided (3 tbsp (37g), 4 tbsp (50g))
- 3 large egg yolks
- 1/3 c whole milk (80g)
- 2 tbsp brewed coffee
- 2 tbsp flour
- 2 tbsp dutch cocoa powder, sifted
- 4 oz bittersweet chocolate, coarsely chopped
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 5 large egg whites, room temp
- 3/4 tsp salt

## Procedure

1. Grease 4 ramekins with butter, using upward strokes with a brush along the sides. Dust with sugar and set aside. Preheat the over to 425F.
2. In a medium heatproof bowl, blanch two of the egg yolks with 3 tbsp of sugar. Slowly stream in milk and coffee while whisking.
3. Add in the flour and cocoa powder and whisk until the mixture has no lumps.
4. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water and continuously whisk until the mixture reaches 170-180F.
5. Take the bowl off the heat and stir in the chocolate. Add in the remaining egg yolk and vanilla extract and mix to combine.
6. In a clean glass or metal mixing bowl, mix the egg white on low speed. Increase to medium speed and continue to beat until white and frothing. Slowly stream in the remaining 4 tbsp of sugar and increase speed to high, beating until stiff peaks emerge.
7. Add 1/3 of the egg white mixture to the chocolate mixture and fold until smooth. Slowly fold in the remaining egg white in two additions, stopping once barely any white streaks remain.
8. Spoon batter into the prepared ramekins, filling them to the top. Level and smooth out the tops, and run your finger around the inside lip of each ramekin. Dust the tops with granulated sugar.
9. Set ramekins on a rimmed baking sheet and place into the oven, lowering the temperature to 375F. Bake until the souffles have risen and wobble slightly, 15-20 minutes. Serve immediately.
